Cindy Boling & Lily

Cindy Boling is one of the warmest, most compassionate and determined people you'll ever meet.

A police officer arrived at her home, the wrong address on a two week old, reported copper theft, and he shot and killed Lily, Cindy's border collie, forever changing Cindy's life. She still mourns for Lily's loss, but that loss has given her a mission: to prevent this from happening to other families. She is presently pursuing the passage of bill she wrote requiring mandatory training for police officers on dog encounters.

Cheye Calvo and Peyton & Chase

A natural leader, Cheye is a five-term mayor of his town, and when tragedy came to his door, he threw himself into changing Maryland law to protect others.

Shockingly, officers didn't even know Cheye Calvo was his town's mayor when SWAT raided his home, killing his two black labs. Cheye was innocent of any wrong doing, but had to fight to clear his name and find justice for Peyton and Chase. He won a lawsuit that required police training and record keeping on the number of SWAT raids and their impact in Maryland.

Matthew and Kelley Rodriguez & Jane

Matthew and Kelley Rodriguez, a young couple with a newborn baby, will not stop until they find justice for Jane, their rescued pitt.

Matthew and Kelley were heartbroken when Jane was shot by police officers responding to an accidental 911 call. Matthew was away on a charity bike ride and Kelley was upstairs nursing her newborn son when police responded to an accidental 911 call by her young niece. Kelley called the department, informing them it was an accident, yet, some hours later, officers arrived at their home. Within a matter of seconds, the two officers, who were both unharmed, shot and killed Jane.

Roger and Sandi Jenkins & Brandi

Roger and Sandi won a huge victory in court for their chocolate lab Brandi, but with appeals haven't seen a penny to pay for Brandi's expensive pain medication.

Roger opened the door one frosty Maryland morning to officers asking if he'd seen his adult son. He told them to wait at the door while he put the dogs away. Not heeding his request the officers started walking around the home, which attracted the dogs in the back. Officers were caught on video as they shot and nearly killed Brandi, who was several feet away from them and not advancing. After winning a landmark lawsuit against the police department, Roger and Sandi are still fighting through the appeals process to ensure Brandi has the medication to help the constant pain she endures from the bullet shards still in her body.

Michael Paxton & Cisco

One afternoon, Michael was in his backyard playing Frisbee with Cisco, and moments later, he was holding the lifeless body of his dog in his arms.

An officer arrived at Michael's apartment, a wrong address on a domestic dispute call. He walked up the driveway gun drawn and pointed at Michael. When Cisco came around from the backyard, the officer, though unharmed, shot and killed him, claiming he'd been attacked. Michael was devastated, but he and his friends launched a social media campaign that drew the attention of the nation, resulting in over 200,000 followers on his Justice for Cisco page.

Ella Shelton & Patches

Sometimes a reminiscent Ella still opens the screen door and calls for Patches, even though she knows Patches, her 12 lbs. Jack Russell terrier, is buried near her home after being shot by a 300 lbs. policeman.

Ella and Patches were constant companions, watching television together, spending their lives together. One day her son's parole officer, who'd been to their home more than 15 times before and met Patches on each occasion, fired his weapon at the twelve pound dog, fatally wounding it. Ella, an Army veteran, believes that what happened to Patches was wrong and wants to ensure others don't have to experience the same trauma her family endured.
